# Day 2 – Networking Fundamentals

## What I studied
- Packets and frames
- TCP/IP three-way handshake
- Practical TCP handshake lab
- UDP/IP
- Ports 101 practical

## What I learned
- Packets and frames are how data is broken down and moved across networks.
- TCP uses a three-way handshake before communication starts.
- UDP does not use a handshake and is faster, but less reliable.
- Ports help identify which service or application network traffic is meant for.
- Common ports are important for cybersecurity because attackers and defenders both look at open services.

## Key takeaway
Networking is the foundation of cybersecurity. Understanding packets, protocols, and ports helps explain how systems communicate and how attackers find entry points.
